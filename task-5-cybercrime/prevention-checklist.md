-------------------------------------------------------------------
Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
-------------------------------------------------------------------
General Online Safety:
--------------------------------------------------------------------

Use strong, unique passwords for each account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols); consider a password manager.

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.

Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments from emails, WhatsApp, or Telegram.

Verify unknown friend/follow requests—fake profiles are commonly used for scams or data theft.

Do not share personal details (Aadhaar, OTPs, passwords, college IDs) on untrusted websites or calls.

Always log out from public/shared computers and avoid saving passwords on them.

Keep your phone and laptop updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.

Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi; avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on open networks.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Financial / UPI Safety:
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank or support team.

Do not scan unknown QR codes or approve “collect requests” without verifying the sender—receiving money does NOT require entering a PIN.

Double-check payment details (name, UPI ID) before sending money, especially for online purchases or rentals.

If Something Goes Wrong:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediately report fraud at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).

Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 as soon as possible to increase chances of recovering lost money.

Staying alert and informed is your strongest defense against cyber threats.
